ANNOTATORS NEEDED

FOR A RESEARCH PROJECT ABOUT META-THERAPY
 

The Purpose
 

Meta-therapy refers to the time-tested clinical dialogues that help voice therapy patients meet their goals more effectively and efficiently. Meta-therapy is thought to be essential to successful voice therapy. However, it lacks a formal evidence base, which prevents it from being systematically studied and taught during educational training. To further clarify and define meta-therapy, we are conducting a study that measures its use by voice-specialized SLPs during voice therapy. This study was approved by the University of Pittsburgh IRB (STUDY22030088).

 

For this step of the project – the one we need your help with – we need 6 expert and 6 novice voice clinicians to  manually annotate clinician dialogue in audio recordings of real-life voice therapy sessions.
 

What will I do?
 

After a one-hour training session, you will work with the transcripts and audio files of pre-recorded voice therapy sessions for one patient-clinician dyad. You will identify the different treatment modalities (direct treatment, indirect treatment, etc, including meta-therapy) of the clinician’s verbal interventions. You will be asked to annotate a total of 4 therapy sessions of this dyad, for an estimated total duration of 6 hours of annotation. One session will be annotated twice, to verify intra-annotator reliability.

 

After all annotations are complete, the annotators will convene with PI Helou as a group to discuss the current model of meta-therapy, and to propose and discuss improvements to the model. Points of confusion and disagreement across annotators will be discussed until consensus is reached about the best possible framework of meta-therapy in voice therapy. 
 

What’s in it for me?
 

Annotators will not receive monetary compensation. However, all annotators who participate in the consensus session will have the option of claiming co-authorship rights on a consensus manuscript resulting from this work. Specifically, your input during the consensus meeting in October will be integrated into a manuscript describing the updated model of meta-therapy.  You will be invited to read and suggest modifications to the manuscript during each iteration leading up to publication, and can elect to be listed as a co-author on the publication. Your input is valuable to this work!
